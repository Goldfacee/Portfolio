Research

This section highlights research work I have contributed to in autonomous drones, energy-efficient flight, and intelligent path planning.

EcoFlight: Low-Energy Path Finding Through Obstacles for Autonomous Sensing Drones

EcoFlight introduces an energy-aware path planning algorithm for autonomous drones navigating 3D environments with obstacles. Instead of optimizing only for shortest distance, the algorithm accounts for energy costs associated with movement, altitude change, and traversal behavior.

Simulation results demonstrate that EcoFlight consistently finds lower-energy routes in obstacle-dense environments compared to traditional path-planning approaches.

Authors: Jordan Leyva, Nahim J. Moran Vera, Yihan Xu, Adrien Durasno, Christopher U. Romero, Tendai Chimuka, Gabriel O. Huezo Ramirez, Ziqian Dong, Roberto Rojas-Cessa

Current / Future Work

This research paper is currently based on simulation results. Our ongoing goal is to extend this work into a real-world system by designing and building an autonomous drone platform capable of implementing the EcoFlight algorithm. By integrating the algorithm into an onboard flight controller, we aim to evaluate energy-aware navigation in real flight conditions.
